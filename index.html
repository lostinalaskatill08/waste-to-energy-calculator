Waste-to-Energy Calculator (Multi-Pillar)

Assumptions Used in the Calculator (Expanded for Biomass Gasification & Pyrolysis):

  • Each person generates per year:
    • 435 kg of food waste
    • 500 kg of plastic/MSW
    • 50 kg of human waste (sewage biosolids)
    • 120 kg of agricultural/forestry waste (applicable to U.S. city yard/woody waste)
  • Anaerobic Digestion for food + human waste → Biogas (methane).
  • H-Power Incineration for non-recyclable plastics/MSW → Electricity.
  • Biomass Gasification for half of the agro/forestry waste → Syngas → Electricity.
  • Biomass Pyrolysis for the other half → Biochar (+ some pyrolytic gas/oil).
  • Energy Conversion Rates:
    • **100 m³ methane per ton** of food/human waste.
    • **10 kWh per m³ of methane**.
    • **0.42 MWh per ton of MSW incinerated**.
    • **1.25 MWh per ton of biomass gasified** (syngas → electricity, net).
    • **25% biochar yield** from pyrolysis (by mass) on the portion assigned to pyrolysis.
    • **3.5 tons CO₂ saved** per ton of food/human waste diverted.
    • **0.25 tons of algae** per ton of CO₂ captured (possible synergy from AD or flue gas).
    • **0.6 tons biofuel** per ton of algae produced.
  • Household Energy Consumption: 10,500 kWh/year (average U.S. city estimate).
